The Power Triad: Thoughts, Feelings and Actions

1. How aware are you of the two different opposing "wolves" operating within your mind, one of which leads to pain and a diminished sense of life and the other to a joyous, meaningful, and fulfilling life?

This question is asking about self-awareness and the ability to recognize the presence of both positive and negative forces within one's mind. The answer would involve reflecting on one's thoughts, emotions, and behaviors to understand how these internal forces influence decision-making and overall well-being.

2. When was the time you feel disappointed by the choice of behavior because you knew that there was a more positive option but you just didn't choose it?

This question is asking about a specific instance where the individual recognized that they made a choice that was not in their best interest. The answer would involve describing the situation, the emotions felt, and any lessons learned from the experience.

3. What ways or techniques or exercises do you use to strengthen yourself so as to increase its potency to choose and hence control your life?

This question is asking about strategies or practices that help the individual become more self- aware, resilient, and capable of making positive choices. The answer would involve describing techniques such as meditation, journaling, or seeking support from friends, family, or a therapist.

4. In what specific ways do you feed the negative wolf?

This question is asking about behaviors or thought patterns that contribute to the growth of negative emotions or thoughts. The answer would involve describing situations or behaviors that trigger negative emotions or thoughts, such as engaging in gossip, dwelling on past mistakes, or avoiding challenging situations.

5. In what specific ways do you use to feed the positive wolf?

This question is asking about behaviors or thought patterns that contribute to the growth of positive emotions or thoughts. The answer would involve describing situations or behaviors that trigger positive emotions or thoughts, such as engaging in acts of kindness, practicing gratitude, or setting and achieving personal goals.
